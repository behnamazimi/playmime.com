## Pick a platform and format

You can play in‑person around a single screen or fully remote over a video call. For remote play, use your favorite meeting tool (Zoom, Google Meet, FaceTime). Share the rules at the top, then choose how you’ll rotate turns:

- **Classic rotation**: One actor per round while their team guesses.
- **Lightning rotation**: Shorter turns, rapid switches to keep momentum.
- **Team captain mode**: A designated caller organizes the order and scores.

Pro tip: Encourage cameras on. Facial expressions are half the fun.

## Set your house rules

Agree on these before the first round:

- No talking, mouthing, writing, or spelling.
- No pointing to on‑screen words or objects in the room.
- Gestures are encouraged: number of words, “sounds like,” movie/book/song icons.
- Decide on time limits (60–90 seconds works well) and scoring (1 point per correct guess).

---

## Prep the tech (fast)

- Test microphones and cameras.
- Remind players to join from a quiet, well‑lit space.
- Ask the current actor to sit far enough from the camera for full‑body gestures.
- Share the PlayMime link so everyone can open the same room or take turns as host.

## Crowd‑pleasing word ideas

Mix difficulty to keep all players engaged:

- Easy: cat, brushing teeth, pizza, dancing, helicopter
- Medium: time traveler, roller coaster, detective, snowstorm
- Tricky: plot twist, renaissance, cliffhanger, documentary

Or skip lists entirely — use PlayMime’s built‑in word banks so you never run out.

## Run your first game

1. Pick a mode in [/play](/en/play).
2. Explain the house rules and gestures.
3. Choose the first actor and start the timer.
4. Score 1 point per correct guess; switch teams each round.
5. After 3–5 rounds, tally the total and celebrate the champions.

Keep the energy high with quick transitions and upbeat music between rounds.

## Keep it fresh with themes

Try theme nights to change the vibe:

- Movie night: only films and famous characters
- Travel night: cities, landmarks, cultural foods
- Sports night: teams, iconic plays, equipment
- Throwback night: ‘90s pop culture, retro tech, old cartoons

Themes make the game feel curated, which boosts excitement and replayability.

## Final tips for unforgettable nights

- Rotate actors so everyone gets camera time.
- Celebrate big reactions and exaggerated movement.
- Use short rounds to prevent downtime.
- Add a funny prize for the winning team (virtual trophy, custom emoji).
