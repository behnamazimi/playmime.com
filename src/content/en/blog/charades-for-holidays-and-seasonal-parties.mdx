Holiday parties are about togetherness — and a little delightful chaos. **Seasonal charades** turns any gathering into a shared story, whether you’re cozying up in winter or grilling in summer. This guide gives you ready‑to‑play prompts for every season, inclusive formats for all ages, and tips to host without breaking a sweat.

Open [/play](/en/play) and play mode; then add a seasonal twist from below.

---

## Winter: cozy, bright, and playful

### Word ideas

Snowman, Sledding, Hot cocoa, Fireplace, Ice skating, Snowball fight, Mittens, Caroling, Gift wrapping, New Year’s countdown

### Format

Warm‑up with easy “Actions,” then shift to winter scenes. Keep turns to 60 seconds — long enough to act with mittens on.

---

## Spring: fresh starts and outdoor energy

### Word ideas

Rain boots, Umbrella, Rainbow, Garden, Picnic, Butterfly, Kite flying, Spring cleaning, Baseball game, Cherry blossoms

### Format

Use “Animals” and “Actions” for kids; add “Travel & Places” for mixed groups. Backyard sessions pair perfectly with a portable speaker.

---

## Summer: sunshine and adventure

### Word ideas

Beach day, Surfing, Lifeguard, Ice cream truck, Fireworks, Road trip, Campfire, Hammock, Sunburn, Snorkeling

### Format

Try “Lightning Gauntlet”: two minutes, two actors, one continuous stream. Keep water nearby — it gets spirited fast.

---

## Fall: harvest, costumes, and game nights

### Word ideas

Pumpkin carving, Leaf pile, Apple picking, Scarecrow, Trick‑or‑treat, Costume party, Corn maze, Football kickoff, Bonfire, Sweater weather

### Format

Blend “Movies & TV” with fall prompts for adults. End with a “Wildcard” round where actors pick any autumn theme.

---

## Hosting tips for seasonal sets

- Build a short playlist to match the theme.
- Provide easy props: scarves, hats, toy instruments.
- Rotate first actors so everyone gets a spotlight moment.
- Keep scoring light; focus on stories and photos.
